My Works

Passively Firmed Happiness Plant

In the backdrop of the digital era, this project takes the attention economy as its starting point. It has created a virtual planting platform aimed at exploring how people's attention manifests in the process of growing plants. By examining how different levels of attention and types of content affect plant growth and morphology, we metaphorically delve into the current state of attention commercialization in society. Such exploration offers viewers an opportunity to reflect on their personal lifestyles and how technology shapes their lives.

Edge Echoes。

This project focuses on the "Sanhe Gods," young people living aimlessly in Shenzhen, China, despite being at an age for ambition and struggle. Their choice, rooted in complex and poignant reasons, mirrors the despairing transition from hope to destitution, akin to Lao She's "Camel Xiangzi," highlighting a loss of identity and resignation.

By reflecting on the living conditions of the Sanhe youth, the project seeks to raise awareness of the deep-seated societal issues of division, inequality, and the struggles of the lower social strata, calling for a deeper understanding and humanitarian concern for these marginalized groups.
